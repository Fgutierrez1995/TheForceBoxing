Meet the Coach

Jerry LaMoore

Jerry Lamoore began his amateur boxing career in 1964. The following year Jerry won the Open Division State Golden Gloves Championship despite being a Novice. In 1966 Jerry joined the Navy where he continued his boxing career as a member of the Navel East Coast Boxing Team in which he became The Atlantic Fleet Champion, The Cruiser and Destroyer Champion of The Atlantic Fleet, Mediterranean Champion as well as the runner up in the All Navy Championship and All East Coast Championship. In 1967, Jerry became the East Coast Champion, Atlantic Fleet Champion and runner up for All Navy Championship. The sum of Jerry’s Amateur career totaled 76 wins, 6 losses, 34 Knockouts and held 8 titles. Jerry became a professional boxer after joining the Milwaukee Bombers in 1969 into 1970 when he hung up his gloves to further his education and work to support his family. Jerry’s career in professional boxing consisted of 4 wins and 1 loss. Jerry owned his own gym at the Christian Center in Milwaukee Wisconsin in 1977 and 1978. Since then Jerry has helped train with Boxing legend and Hall of Famer Curtis Cokes at the Pike Park Boxing Club in Dallas Texas. Jerry Lamoore now owns The Force Boxing in Greenville Texas where he can be seen working with the youth and teaching the life lessons that come with boxing.

“It's not just about Boxing, It’s about life” - Jerry LaMoore
